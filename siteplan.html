WeldPath — Types of Welding

Types of Welding

MIG • TIG • Stick • Flux‑Cored

Home Types of Welding Careers Safety Request Info References

MIG — Gas Metal Arc Welding

  • Best for: General fabrication, production work
  • Pros: Faster, easier to learn, good on thin to medium steel
  • Cons: Sensitive to wind (shielding gas)

TIG — Gas Tungsten Arc Welding

  • Best for: Precision work, thin metals, aluminum & stainless
  • Pros: Clean, strong, precise control
  • Cons: Slower; higher skill requirement

Stick — Shielded Metal Arc Welding

  • Best for: Outdoor/field work, thicker materials
  • Pros: Portable, tolerates dirty/rusty steel
  • Cons: More cleanup (slag), harder on thin metal

Flux‑Cored — FCAW

  • Best for: Heavy fabrication, structural steel
  • Pros: High deposition rates, good outdoors (self‑shielded)
  • Cons: More spatter; wire cost

Essential PPE

  • Auto‑darkening helmet, safety glasses
  • Flame‑resistant jacket and gloves
  • Proper boots; avoid synthetic fabrics

  • Ventilation for fumes and gases
